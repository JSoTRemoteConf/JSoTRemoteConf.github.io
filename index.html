Ah, February.

The holidays will be over, summer conference season will still be a ways off, and, chances are, you will be trapped by ice or snow in your apartment in Bushwick or Portland or wherever you may be.

Why not make some hot chocolate, fire up your laptop, and hang out virtually with a bunch of people who are just as into JavaScript on hardware as you are?

Introducing the first ever JavaScript on Things Remote Conference.

  • Who: People from all over the world who are working on bringing JavaScript to the Internet of Things, whether with johnny-five, Espruino, Kinoma, or something else.
  • What: A chance to get a "state of the union" on JSoT endeavors, learn new tricks, and attend some neat workshops for a variety of skill levels.
  • Where: The comfort of your own home or coffee shop, PJs optional. You could also get a group together to stream it in one place.
  • When: February 2017, exact date(s) TBD.
  • Why: Because the JSoT community is still small enough for something like this makes sense. And because traveling with hardware demos can be a pain and requires time away from work and family.
  • Cost: TBD, but the goal is to make this accessible to a wide variety of people.

We need your help!

Since this is the first time this is happening, we need lots of help. Here are things we need help with:

  • Planning: If you've got event planning experience, especially with remote events, get in here.
  • Design Skills for Conference Site and Identity: Where are my super talented artists?
  • Logicistcs & Technology: Streaming video, audio, and chat for the event itself.
  • Speakers & Workshops: A CFP will be announced soon! We'll need lecture/keynote style updates on major endeavors and workshops across skill levels.
  • Inclusivity & Accessibility: Even though this is a remote conference, we want to strive to make it accessible to as many people as possible. If you're an expert at that, please jump in.
  • Pizzazz: Just because it's remote doesn't mean it can't be awesome. Events like XOXO, Cascadia, and DinosaurJS have really raised the bar. Let's do something creative with this!

If any of this is up your alley, hit me up on Twitter and I'll get you plugged in.
